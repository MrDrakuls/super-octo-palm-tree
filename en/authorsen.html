Information for Authors

The journal publishes articles by the faculty of higher and secondary educational institutions, master's students, university students, and independent experts.

IMPORTANT RULE

  • The article is published in the author's original edition. The author is responsible for the reliability of the facts, quotes, correct spelling of proper names, titles, formulas, and numbers presented in the article.

  • The Publisher or the editorial board of the Edition reserves the right to retract (recall) the Work from the scientific domain without a statute of limitations for publication in cases of violation by the author of generally accepted norms of scientific ethics.

  • The Edition adheres to the rules generally accepted in the scientific community regarding conflict of interest, violation of publication ethical rules.

Manuscript Formatting Rules

1. Only materials that have not been previously published or submitted for review to other publications are accepted for publication.

2. Recommended material volume: from 7 pages of A4 format.

3. An Antiplagiat system report must be attached (Text originality must be no less than 75%).

4. Materials are submitted in the following form:

TEXT

  • Text format: Microsoft Word (*.doc, *.docx)
  • Orientation: Portrait.
  • Margins: 20 mm on all sides.
  • Line spacing: 1.5 lines (semi-double).
  • Paragraph indent: 10 mm.
  • Font: Times New Roman, size (point) – 14 pt.
  • Formatting: justified (aligned to width).
  • The use of the following is not allowed: page breaks, section breaks, columns, sparse or condensed inter-character spacing, and page numbering.
  • Graphic elements (figures, tables): must be formatted uniformly with the main text of the article, including fonts and sizes. Figures and tables must be separated from the text by an empty line above and below. All tables and figures must be numbered and have references in the text (e.g., Tab. 1, Fig. 1).
    Graphic materials must be presented with a resolution of at least 300 dpi.

BEFORE THE TEXT

  • UDC and LBC (aligned to the left). Find the UDC on the website: teacode.com/online/udc.
  • Author Information: (aligned to the left) — Surname, First Name, Patronymic (full) in Russian (in bold font);
    on the line below — academic degree, academic rank, position;
    on the line below — place of work (study);
    on the line below — email address.
  • Abstract: When compiling the abstract, special attention must be paid to its content, which should briefly reflect the key aspects of the article. The abstract structure must include three main parts: problem statement (or state of the issue), results, and conclusions, each of which must be highlighted with appropriate headings.

    The recommended abstract volume is 100 to 150 words, printed in a 12 pt font, justified alignment.
  • Keywords: separated by commas, justified alignment (5-10 words).
  • The article title, author's full name, email, academic degree, academic rank, position, place of work/study, abstract, and keywords are duplicated in English.

AFTER THE TEXT

  • The reference list is placed at the end of the article under the heading "List of Sources" and is arranged in the strict order in which the sources are mentioned in the text. The list is separated from the main text of the article by an empty line. The list title is formatted in bold font.
  • The list of sources must include at least 10 scientific publications, including articles, monographs, and dissertations. It is recommended to use publications published within the last 5 years. Priority is given to journal publications of the RSCI and HAC level. Textbooks, study guides, abstracts, and popular science websites are not recommended.
  • In the text, references to bibliographic sources are formatted as square brackets containing the source number and, separated by a comma, the page number: [5, p. 115]. **The use of footnotes is not allowed**.
  • The use of automatic numbering is not allowed; when creating the list, numbering is done manually.

The reference list is formatted in accordance with GOST R 7.0.5-2008.
